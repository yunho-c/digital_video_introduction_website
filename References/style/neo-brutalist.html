SPATIAL COMPRESSION

Folding the laundry (Intra-frame compression).

This is basically how JPEGs work. If you have a picture of a blue sky, you don't need to write "blue, blue, blue" 1,000 times.

Instead, the computer says: "x500 Blue Pixels". It groups similar colors into Macroblocks and discards fine detail your eye won't notice.

Key Techniques

  • Discrete Cosine Transform: Math for "smoothing"
  • Chroma Subsampling: Removing color info

TEMPORAL COMPRESSION

Don't repeat yourself. If the background doesn't move, don't save it again.

I

I-Frames

The whole picture. Expensive data-wise. Only happens on scene cuts.

P

P-Frames

Predicted frames. Only saves what changed from the last frame.

B

B-Frames

Bi-directional. Looks forward and backward to guess the middle image.

THE TRANSLATORS

Codecs (Coder-Decoder) are the instruction manuals for these tricks.

H.264 (AVC)

The Classic

The Honda Civic of compression. Reliable, plays on everything from an old iPhone to a toaster. Not efficient enough for 4K.

H.265 (HEVC)

High Efficiency

Twice as smart as H.264. Crucial for 4K/HDR. Licensing it is a nightmare, but it works great.

AV1

The Future

Free, open-source, and incredibly tiny files. Used by YouTube and Netflix. Kills your battery if your phone is old.
